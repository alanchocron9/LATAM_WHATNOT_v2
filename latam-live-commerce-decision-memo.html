The most important thing to understand: you do not need to create a new behavior. The behavior exists, at scale, with zero infrastructure. The platform formalizes what is already happening informally on Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp every day.

Three Documented Behaviors

1. Claim Commerce Is the Dominant Live Format

The "comment to claim" mechanic — buyers typing "LO QUIERO" to reserve an item — is observable as the standard format on Instagram Live and Facebook Live across LATAM today. A platform that replaces the comment with a one-tap button is a workflow upgrade to a trained behavior, not the introduction of a new concept.

2. WhatsApp Is Already the Commerce Infrastructure

WhatsApp penetration in Brazil exceeds 95% of smartphone users. Source: Future Market Insights 2026. Colombia follows comparable patterns. Nequi has 24M+ users — more than half of Colombia's adult population. Source: Fintech News America 2025. Digital wallet transfers grew 231% in H2 2024. Source: Colombia Financial Superintendence. PSE recorded 370M+ transactions in Q1 2024 alone. Source: PCMI 2024. The rails exist. What does not exist is a structured platform sitting on top of them.

3. Live Commerce Converts 5–15x Better Than Traditional Ecommerce

Live commerce conversion rates run 9–30% globally versus 2–3% for traditional ecommerce. Source: Firework / Marketing LTB 2025; IRP Commerce benchmarks. H&M reported 40% of live commerce purchases occur within the first 10 minutes of a session. Source: Future Market Insights 2026. Companies report conversion up to 10x higher than conventional ecommerce. Source: Straits Research 2025. This is a documented global phenomenon, not a LATAM-specific projection.

What Is Proven vs. What Is Inferred

Proven: Social commerce in LATAM is large and fast-growing. WhatsApp and Nequi are already the payment and communication rails. Live commerce converts at dramatically higher rates globally. LATAM consumers spend 41 minutes per live session — highest in the world.

Inferred, not precisely sourced: The exact count of informal live sellers in Colombia. The informal GMV flowing through Instagram Live and WhatsApp that is not captured in formal datasets. These are reasonable inferences from the sourced data above but they are inferences.

Section 04
Why a New Platform Can Win

The question is not whether live commerce will be big in LATAM — it will be. The question is whether a new, dedicated platform can carve out a real position. The answer is yes, for structural reasons.

1. Discovery Is the Product Nobody Is Selling

Every seller on Instagram today is hard-capped by her follower count. A seller with 5,000 followers can only sell to those 5,000 people. A live commerce platform with a discovery feed changes her economics entirely — surfacing her to buyers in Bogotá, Barranquilla, and Cali who have never seen her content. That is a 10x expansion of addressable market with no additional effort. It is why sellers switch and why they stay.

2. The Seller Tooling Gap Is Worth Real Money

After every live session, a typical Colombian fashion seller spends 2+ hours on post-session DMs. A platform that automates that in one click — order queue generated automatically, Nequi payment confirmed instantly, bulk shipping labels in one tap — saves hours per week. That operational upgrade creates real loyalty. Whatnot's month-over-month seller retention is 80% in 2025, up 18% YoY. Source: Sacra 2025. That is what happens when you make sellers meaningfully more money with less effort.

3. Medellín Is the Fashion Supply Capital of LATAM

Medellín produces 45% of Colombia's textiles from 10,000+ factories. Source: Medellín Advisors / Colombia One. Colombiamoda brings 12,000 fashion buyers to one location annually. Source: Business of Fashion 2024. Lingerie is Colombia's second-largest apparel export after denim. Source: California Apparel News. Fashion is the #1 live commerce category globally at 21.4% market share. Beauty accounts for 51% of total live shopping sales globally. Source: Grand View Research / QuickMarketPitch 2025. The seller supply problem that kills marketplace cold starts does not exist here.

4. Colombia's Payment Infrastructure Is Ready and Growing

Nequi: 24M+ users, near-zero transaction cost, instant settlement. Source: Fintech News America 2025. Digital wallet transfers: +231% in H2 2024. Source: Colombia Financial Superintendence. PSE: 370M+ transactions in Q1 2024 alone. Source: PCMI 2024. Bre-B — Colombia's PIX equivalent — launching in 2025. Source: PCMI 2025. Brazil's PIX transformed ecommerce conversion rates nationally after launch. Bre-B is positioned to do the same in Colombia. Time the marketing push to ride that adoption curve.

5. Colombia Is the R&D Phase for Brazil's $55.7B Market

Brazil social commerce: $15.6B in 2025, $55.7B by 2030, 29% CAGR. Source: Mordor Intelligence 2025. WhatsApp in Brazil: 95%+ of smartphone users. Source: Future Market Insights 2026. But entering Brazil cold at seed, against TikTok Shop which hit $25.7M GMV in its first 3 months there, is expensive. Entering at Series A with a working Colombian playbook and real seller data is materially better. Colombia at $1–2M proves the model. Brazil at $12–20M scales it.

Why It Wins

It wins by serving a specific seller segment TikTok and Instagram actively ignore — the informal micro-seller doing live commerce from her home, managing her business on WhatsApp, generating real revenue with no professional infrastructure. That seller is the market. A platform built for her, in a city where she is densely concentrated, with payment rails that work and a fashion supply chain unmatched in the region, has a genuine and defensible first position.

Section 06
How to Prove This Before Going All In

The goal is to answer one question with real data: do sellers earn more on this platform than on Instagram, and do they stay? If yes, the business is real. These steps are ordered cheapest to most committed.

The Governing Logic

Five data points drive the entire financial model and cannot be reliably estimated from a desk: average viewers per session, real AOV, viewer-to-claim conversion, seller 90-day retention, and COD non-collection rate. These steps generate all five. Do not raise a Pre-Series A before having real numbers on all five.

Step 1 — Talk to 20 Sellers Before Building Anything ($0, 2–3 weeks)

Before writing a line of code, have direct conversations with 20 sellers currently doing live selling on Instagram or Facebook in Medellín and Bogotá. The goal is not to pitch them. It is to understand their actual workflow and pain. Key questions: orders per session, post-session management time, AOV, payment method mix, and whether they'd switch for order automation + new buyer discovery.

Green light: 15/20 describe the same core pain and express clear interest.

Step 2 — Run 10 Live Sessions Manually (Minimal Cost, 4–6 weeks)

No product. Use a basic livestream, a Google Form for order capture, Nequi for payment. This is the "Wizard of Oz" test. It gives you the five critical data points — avg viewers per session, viewer-to-claim conversion, real AOV, payment mix, and seller willingness to return — that you cannot get any other way.

Green light: Sellers generate more orders per session than on their current Instagram Lives. Conversion above 7%. Sellers want to repeat.

Step 3 — Build MVP, 30 Days With 15 Sellers ($50–150K)

Build the smallest possible functional product: live stream, one-tap RECLAMAR, Nequi/PSE/COD, auto order queue, shipping label generation. Nothing else. 15 sellers, 30 days. Core question: do sellers earn more per session than on Instagram?

Green light: Sellers earning more, payment completion above 75%, 10 of 15 run a second session without being asked.

Step 4 — 90-Day Retention (Most Important Metric)

This cannot be rushed. Track the Month 1 cohort through Day 90. What % is still active? Is per-session GMV growing or declining? What are churned sellers saying? For context: Whatnot's month-over-month seller retention is 80% in 2025. Source: Sacra 2025. Target 55%+ at 90 days before raising Pre-Series A.

Green light: 55%+ of Month 1 cohort still active at Day 90. GMV per session flat or growing.

What "Going All In" Means

Going all in means raising Pre-Series A ($3–6M) and scaling from 50 sellers to 200+ across 4 Colombian cities. That decision should only be made after all four green light signals are confirmed. The Colombia-first approach exists precisely because you can reach all four answers for $500K–$1.5M before committing the capital Brazil requires.

Section 07
The Verdict
Go / No-Go

Go. The market is large, the behavior is proven, the infrastructure gap is real, and the window in Colombia is open right now. This is worth pursuing — starting in Medellín, with fashion and beauty, on seed capital that is modest relative to the size of the opportunity if it proves out.

The Case For

  • The model is proven globally at scale. TikTok Shop: $33B GMV 2024, $66B projected 2025. Whatnot: $8B GMV at $11.5B valuation. Source: Resourcera / Sacra / CapitalG 2025.
  • LATAM consumers are the most engaged live commerce audience in the world. 41 minutes average session time, higher than China and the US. Cultural fit, not a marketing problem. Source: electroiq.com 2025.
  • The behavior is trained, not to be created. Informal live sellers are already doing this daily. The platform is the infrastructure upgrade.
  • Colombia's payment infrastructure is ready. Nequi at 24M users, +231% wallet growth, Bre-B launching. Source: Colombia Financial Superintendence / PCMI 2025.
  • TikTok Shop Colombia is still in beta. The window to establish seller lock-in before full TikTok rollout is open now. It will not be in 18 months.
  • Colombia is capital-efficient proof of concept. $500K–$1.5M gets you to the answer. Brazil at Series A with real data is a fundable, credible raise.

The Case Against

  • TikTok is coming. It grew US monthly GMV from $15M to $1.1B in 24 months. Source: Charm.io 2025. Once fully live in Colombia, seller recruitment gets harder.
  • Marketplace cold starts are operationally demanding. Getting the first 50 sellers and buyers simultaneously requires hands-on execution.
  • Colombia's GMV milestones are modest. $2M monthly GMV at Month 12 is real but not large. This is a proof-of-concept phase, not a revenue phase.

What Makes It Succeed vs. Fail

Makes It Work

  • First 50 sellers recruited through warm introductions, not cold outreach
  • Sellers visibly earn more per session than on Instagram within 60 days
  • Discovery feed surfaces sellers to new buyers from Day 1
  • COD + Nequi both supported — no buyer segment excluded
  • Move before TikTok's full Colombian rollout

Watch Closely

  • TikTok Shop Colombia launch timeline — any acceleration changes everything
  • Seller retention at 90 days — single most important early metric
  • Average viewers per session — discovery engine's report card
  • COD non-collection rate — Colombia is still heavily cash-based
  • Bre-B payment launch — ride that adoption curve

What Kills It

  • Sellers onboard but don't go live — platform stays empty
  • TikTok fully launches before seller lock-in is established
  • Viewer counts don't grow — discovery engine not working
  • Launching Brazil and Colombia simultaneously — splits focus
  • Waiving take rate to acquire any seller

Immediate Next Step

The move that costs zero and proves the most: identify 20 sellers in Medellín who are currently doing Instagram Live selling and are reachable through trusted introductions. Have conversations before building anything. That list is the foundation. Everything else follows from getting those conversations right.
